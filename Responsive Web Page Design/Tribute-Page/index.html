About Spider-Man

Spider-Man is a fictional superhero created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko. He first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15 in 1962, published by Marvel Comics. The character’s alter ego, Peter Parker, is a young man who gains spider-like abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider.

Origin Story

Peter Parker lives with his Aunt May and Uncle Ben in Queens, New York. After gaining superpowers, Peter initially uses them for personal gain. However, after the tragic death of his Uncle Ben — which he could have prevented — Peter learns that "with great power comes great responsibility."

Powers and Abilities

  • Wall-crawling
  • Superhuman strength, speed, and agility
  • Spider-sense (danger detection)
  • Web-shooters he built himself
  • Brilliant scientific mind

Legacy

Spider-Man is one of the most iconic superheroes in the world. He has appeared in countless comic books, television series, and blockbuster films. His struggles to balance heroism with personal life make him a relatable and enduring character.
